## Act 1: Terminology
- **Advantage**: You roll the affected roll two times and use the highest result. 
- **Ahn (₳)**: Money of the City, the smallest credit you will find is 1,000 ahn.
- **Augment**: Equipment that defines the passive capabilities of the character, allowing them to do feats usually impossible without it.
Challenge Roll: A roll of [2d6+Stat] with the appropriate stat for the situation. A Fortitude Challenge Roll would demand for the character to roll 2d6+Fortitude.
- **Charm**: A representation of a character’s social capabilities and empathy, capable of influencing others and keeping a good composure.
- **Clash**: Opposed Roll in combat, where characters roll Combat Dices against each other, with the higher result being a Clash Win and the lower result being a Clash Lose.
- **Condition**: An effect currently afflicting the Character, said effects can be positive or negative.
- **Combat Dices**: Dices used for combat, starting at 1d10+0 but being able to be modified by Effects, Weapon/Outfit Types, and others. They divide in Offensive and Defensive.
- **Combat Round**: An approximately six seconds of game time (depending on the GM), imposed during combat.
- **Combat Turn**: Active time of one Character or Element in combat. During their Combat Turn, characters can use their Actions and On Your Turn Reactions.
- **Defensive Dice**: Dice used during Reactions using Block or Evade.
- **Dice (xDy)**: Dice rolls are represented by xDy, the x represents how many dice you need to roll, and the y how many faces that dice (known as Dice Max) have, you sum up all dice rolled to find your result. For example, all Challenge Rolls are 2d6, that means, roll two dice of six faces each one and sum their results for the final result.
- **Dice Max**: A modifier increasing the number of faces that a dice has. For example, a D6 with +3 Dice Max is in total a D9.
- **Dice Power**: A modifier applied to the dice after rolling. For example, +3 Dice Power on a D6 means that you roll the D6 and add three to the result, normally noted as D6+3
- **Difficulty Modifier**: A bonus or penalty applied to a roll. A Difficulty Modifier of   -3 means that you need to subtract three from the final result.
- **Disadvantage**: You roll the affected roll two times and use the lowest result.
- **Effect**: Positive or Negative modifier applied on the Character. For more information, read the Effect List.
- **Effect Points (EP)**: Points used to purchase positive Effects; More of these can be earned by taking negative Effect Points.
- **Fortitude**: A representation of a character's physical prowess and endurance, capable of sustaining damage and doing hard physical labor.
- **Game Master (GM)**: Person in charge of guiding the Players, coming up with an adventure, and presenting the world where Characters occur, interpreting NPCs and rolling for them.
- **Hit Points (HP)**: A measure of relative health of a character, its reduction representing wounds. For more information of when a PC hits 0 HP, read the Bleed Out, Scarred Condition, and Dying section.
- **Temporary Hit Points (THP)**: A measure of alternative health of a character, its reduction representing alternative sources (i.e. armor, energy, etc). If a character has Temporary Hit Points, they are reduced before HP. In addition, THP cannot stack. If you would gain THP from two or more different sources, you only benefit from one Source of your choosing.
- **Insight**: A representation of a character’s awareness of their surroundings, capable of noticing information by any sense.
- **Justice**: A representation of a character's boldness and dexterity, capable of acting quickly and precise.
- **Level**: Numerical value that represents the level of experience of the character.
- **Light**: A resource used for special actions like Skills that increases based on the Character Rank.
- **Luck Roll**: A roll of 2d6 without any modifiers.
- **Movement**: Number of SQRs that a character can move. By default, they can only move 6 SQRs.
- **Non-Player Character (NPC)**: A character or creature that is interpreted and controlled by the GM instead of a player.
- **Offensive Dice**: Dice used during Attack and Counter, corresponding to Slash, Pierce and Blunt.
- **One Sided**: An Action against a Character that doesn’t proc a Clash.
- **Opposed Roll**: Roll between two or more characters where the highest roll is considered the winner instead of needing to surpass a threshold determined by the GM.
- **Outfit**: Equipment that defines the defensive capabilities of the character, giving them their Defensive Dice.
- **Panic**: A state reached at 0 SP that forces the character to act in a certain way depending on their Stats.
- **Player Character (PC)**: Character that is interpreted and controlled by a player.
- **Prudence**: A representation of a character’s knowledge and cognitive understanding of phenomena, capable of knowing multiple information and doing intellectual labor.
- **Rank**: Numerical value that represents the tier of the character or their equipment. Weapons, Outfits, Tools, Augment, and Skills have Rank. 
- **Sanity Points (SP)**: A measure of the immediate mental wellbeing of a PC, its reduction represents stress and fear. Usually when a character reaches 0 SP, they enter in Panic.
- **Skill**: Special abilities that a character can do using Light.
- **Speed**: Value of initiative of the character, normally rolled at the start of combat. Higher Speed characters act first in the Round.
- **Square (SQR)**: Square in a battlemap, normally having a size of 5 feet or 1.5 meters. 
- **Stack**: Intensity of a Status Effect.
- **Staggered**: A state reached at 0 ST that stops the capacity of the character to act for one turn and reduces all their Resistances to Fatal.
- **Stagger Threshold (ST)**: A measure of relative combat posture of a character, its reduction represents unawareness and shock. Usually when a Character reaches 0 ST, they are Staggered.
- **Stat Block**: All the information relevant for Equipment like Weapons or Outfits, giving their Rank, EP, Effects and Dices.
- **Stats**: A character's intrinsic attributes (Fortitude, Prudence, Justice, Charm, Insight, Temperance).
- **Status Effect**: An effect that is applied to a character, affecting said character until it is resolved. Each effect has their own rules and ways to be resolved. For more information, read the Effect List.
- **Temperance**: A representation of a character’s steadfastness and composure, capable of working with concentration and not being influenced.
- **Tool**: Equipment that serves as support for the character, giving them limited but different tools to solve problems.
- **Weapon**: Equipment that defines the offensive capabilities of the character, giving them their Offensive Dice.

### Character Creation
Before filling the rest of your sheet, it is advised that you talk with your GM about the expectations & desires, of not only the game, but the rest of the players at the table. 

This helps set a standard of what kind of tone people want at the table. Communication is key, as having a serious character or over the top character in the wrong setting of your GM curated narrative can derail everyone's playtime easily.

The process consists of 5 steps:
1. Stats
2. Derived Attributes
3. Equipment Crafting
4. Initial Wealth
5. Finishing details

### Step 1: Stats
The first thing that defines your character are their stats, as they define their narrative and combat capabilities. Your character has ***six*** Stats that determine different attributes of the character.

#### Fortitude: Physical Prowess
Fortitude represents physical prowess and the capacity to endure harm. It affects the following things:
- Each point of Fortitude increases your HP by `8`. [That starts at `64 + (32*Rank)`]
- Fortitude is also used for the Grappling Maneuver, both in starting it and defending from it.
- Fortitude Challenge Rolls involve acts related to raw strength and physicality, like kicking down a door, doing a long climb or winning an arm-wrestling match.

#### Prudence: Intellectual Prowess
Prudence represents the cognitive knowledge and the capacity of analysis of the character. It affects the following things:
- Each point of Prudence increases your SP by `3`. [That starts at `15`]
- Prudence is added to the amount of Light a character can recover through enemy stagger and takedowns in combat (normally Rank)
- Prudence Challenge Rolls involve acts related to raw cognitive challenges and knowledge, like recalling information on a subject, studying something scientifically, or analyzing the enemy’s tactics.

#### Justice: Speed and Boldness
Justice represents the capacity of the character to act quickly and without fear. It affects the following things:
- Justice is added to the character Speed (normally `1d6`)
- Justice is added to the extra SQRs received by the Dash Action (normally `3`).
- Justice is added to the number of Stacks the Reduce Status Effect Action affects (normally `2*Rank` of the Character)
- Justice Challenge Rolls involve acts of speed and quick reaction, like running to catch someone, take quick control of a vehicle, or driving.

#### Charm: Social Capabilities
Charm represents the social awareness and finesse of the character to enchant others. It affects the following things:
- Each point of Charm increases your ST by `4` [That starts at `20 + (4*Rank)`]
- Charm Challenge Rolls involve acts of social action, like convincing a guard of a lie, calming a person under stress, or doing a musical presentation.

#### Insight: Awareness and attention
Insight represents the capacity of the character to receive information through their senses. It affects the following things:
- Insight is added to the Dice Power of Evade. [That starts at `1d12+0`]
- Insight Challenge Rolls involve acts of detection and searching, like looking for a target in a crowd, noticing an ambush, or reading the intentions of someone.

#### Temperance: Restraint and Composure
Temperance represents the capacity of the character to act cautiously and control their own emotions. It affects the following things:
- Temperance is added to the Dice Power of Block. [That starts at `1d10+0`]
- Temperance Challenge Rolls involve acts of self-control and delicate coordination, like hiding one's emotions, doing crafts like pottery, or stealing something without noticing.

#### Buying Stats
Your character begins with a set number of stats to allocate depending on their rank:

| Rank | Stat Points |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| 0 | 6 |
| 1 | 12 |
| 2 | 18 |
| 3 | 24 |
| 4 | 30 |
| 5 | 36 |
| EX | 42 |

At your GM discretion, you can do Stat Trading by reducing one stat to increase another, with the following restrictions:
- You can’t have a stat below `-1`.
- You can’t have a stat higher than your [`Rank+2`]

So, even if the GM allows for Stat Trading, at Rank 1 you can’t have a stat above 3.

### Step 2: Derived Attributes
Once your Stats are ready, you need to calculate your derived Attributes, the Sheet does it automatically, but is useful if you know the formulas and what each one represent:

#### Health Points (HP)
  - **Definition**: Represents your capacity to endure damage before dying.
  - **Formula**: [`64 + (Fortitude*8) + (Rank*32)`]

#### Stagger Threshold (ST)
- **Definition**: Represents your capacity to endure damage before being exposed.
- **Formula**: [`20 + (Charm*4) + (Rank*4)`]

#### Sanity Points (SP): 
- **Definition**: Represents your capacity to endure mental hardship before panicking.
- **Formula**: [`15 + (Prudence*3)`]

#### Light:	
- **Definition**: Resource used for fueling your special Skills.
- **Formula**: [`3+Rank`]

#### Attack Modifier: 
- **Definition**: Added as Dice Power to Attack Rolls
- **Formula**: [`Rank`]

#### Evade Modifier: 
- **Definition**: Added as Dice Power to your Evade Rolls
- **Formula**: [`Insight`]

#### Block Modifier:
- **Definition**: Added as Dice Power to your Block Rolls
- **Formula**: [`Temperance`]

#### Equipment Rank Limit:
- **Definition**: The maximum Rank of equipment you can use.
- **Formula**: [`Rank`] or [`Rank+1`] with disadvantage

#### Tool Slots:
- **Definition**: Maximum number of Tools a character can carry with them.
- **Formula**: `4`

### Step 3: Equipment Crafting and Skill Crafting
By default, all equipment and skills in PM TTRPG are custom made. This means that you need to create the stat block of your Equipment and Skills by yourself, without a premade list for them.

Further details are gone over in Act 3: Effects & Skills– the main thing to note is, the higher Rank a piece of equipment or Skill is, the stronger and more varied it can be. 

#### Equipment
Rank 1 Characters start out with the following:
- One Rank 1 Weapon.
- One Rank 1 Outfit.
An entire Rank 1 Augment (8 Positive & 4 Negative Points) if desired.
Higher Rank characters should start out with appropriate equipment to match their Rank, and are recommended to be supplied with options such as additional Weapons or Tools as is applicable.

#### Skills
For Rank 1 Characters, they start out with two Skills of Rank 1.
For higher Rank Characters, create two Skills of their Rank, and the remaining four Skills of the Rank below– a Rank 3 Character, for example, should have two Rank 3 Skills and four Rank 2 Skills.

This ruling can be modified depending on your GM– this is merely the standard as a character progresses through levels on typical.

#### Consideration: Appearance in The City
It is important to consider that, in The City, appearances are deceitful, and equipment can look and function totally different from what one might expect. A briefcase can be as or more dangerous than any two handed sword, and a formal dress can offer great protection against harm, even when getting hit in parts that it doesn’t cover.

So, be creative and make whatever sorts of equipment your heart tells you. Don’t be afraid of using an umbrella as a two-handed slash weapon or saying that your metal armor has technology that compensates for the normal vulnerability to blunt weapons.

### Step 5: Finishing Touches
Once your character is mechanically finished, you need to fill in their narrative! The BioSheet allows you to put:
- **Name**: The City has *no* naming conventions, names of all our world are present, to the real ones -like Roland, Oliver or Angela- to more fantastical ones -Malkuth or Elijah-. Even nicknames are somewhat common and valid in The City.
- **Occupation**: This is normally informed by your campaign, as mixed works on The City are very rare. Talk with your GM about what occupations are available in the game. Fixer, Syndicate Thug, Meister, Trainee or even Wing Employee are all equally valid if the GM is informed!
- **Age**: By City standards, young people should be separated from violence up to their 20s. Fixer requirements respect this, and you need at least 20 years to be a Fixer, but nothing stops Cults, Workshops and Syndicates from recruiting younger people. Talk with your GM about the expected age range, we recommend characters to be at least 16 years old.
- **Height**: How tall is your character? A 2-meter tower or a small king of 1.50?
- **Birthplace and Residence**: The City is still an interconnected City, even if their frontiers are far and wide. Select a District where you were born and one where you live. They probably would be the same, but nothing stops your character from migrating to another District for better job opportunities.
- **Description**: How do they look? Especially if you use Picrews or faceclaims, what is different between the art and the real character? Do they have a scar? Maybe they are always smiling? Or have they lost the light in their eyes?
- **Background**: A common question is “How did you come here?” What is the story of your character that guides them to this campaign? Talk with your GM to come with something that can be useful for the game. In a Fixer-focused campaign, what motivates your character to become a Fixer? In a campaign about a Syndicate looking for vengeance, what was so important that motivates that vengeance?
- **Personality**: A simple guideline for how people, especially your GM, should expect your character to act. Are they arrogant and think they’re always right? A little silly, appearing like a bit of an airhead? Maybe a professional with a curiosity for something in specific?
- **Ahn**: The money the character has. You will need it in this economy.
- **Notes, Relationships and Notable History**: If you want to be more in depth with your character, track your relationships with other PCs and NPCs, or simply keep a record of your exploits in the campaign, any of that can be elaborated upon in these sections.

Once all the finishing touches are done, your character is ready to play!

## Act 3: Effects and Skills
### Basics: Effects and Effect Points
The system is built around the **Effect System**, which allows you to fully customize your Equipment and Skills as you like. 

You utilize the Effect System to add Effects that enhance the performance and attributes of a piece of Equipment and Skills. These can include increasing the odds of winning a Clash, Inflicting different Status Effects, and much more.

How many Effects a piece of Equipment or a Skill can have is limited by their Effect Points according to their form and Rank. All Effects have an associated Cost, occupying the spaces given by the Effect Points.

Effects can be designated as negative or otherwise have a negative cost, which increases the maximum Effect Points a piece of Equipment or Skill can use. These negatives can be taken up to the initial Effect Points present on the stat block. For example, if a Rank 2 Weapon has 6 EP, it can have at most 6 negative EP allotted, for a maximum 12 EP filled out– 6 negative points, and 12 positive points.

All Effects and their specific costs can be read in the Effect List.


The value of EP for each Rank of Equipment or Skill is listed in the following table:

| **Rank** | Weapon EP | Outfit EP | Augment EP | Skill EP | Handless EP | 1H Tool EP | 2H Tool EP |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| **-1** | 0 | 0 | --- | --- | --- | 0 | 2 |
| **0** | 2 | 2 | 0 | 2 | 0 | 2 | 2 |
| **1** | 4 | 4 | 4 | 4 | 2 | 4 | 4 |
| **2** | 6 | 6 | 8 | 6 | 4 | 6 | 6 |
| **3** | 8 | 8 | 12 | 8 | 6 | 8 | 8 |
| **4** | 10 | 10 | 16 | 10 | 8 | 10 | 10 |
| **5** | 12 | 12 | 20 | 12 | 10 | 12 | 12 |
| **EX** | 14 | 14 | 24 | 14 | 12 | 14 | 14 |
| **Formula** | `(Rank*2)+2` | `(Rank*2)+2` | `Rank*4` | `(Rank*2)+2` | `Rank*2` | `(Rank*2)+2` | `(Rank*2)+4` |

### Skills
Skills represent special maneuvers performed by your character, fueled by Light.
A character can have up to 6 known skills at once, representing different maneuvers or strategies learned through experience and trials.

#### Light
Light is a representation of the power and motivation of the character; at low Light the character is tired and incapable of acting at their best.
A Character can have a maximum of Rank+3 Light normally.
Skills use Light to work, having a minimum cost of 1 Light.
A character’s capacity to regenerate Light will influence how you decide the Light costs for your Skills. The ways to regenerate light are as follows:
- Regen `1` Light when Staggering or Taking Down an opponent. This can only be performed [`Rank+Prudence`] times per combat. This Light can also be given to an ally, acting as if they performed the Stagger or Takedown appropriately.
- Regen `Character Rank` Light when finishing a combat
- Regen `1` Light when Staggered
- Regen `Character Rank` Light after an hour of rest

#### Creating Skills
When creating a Skill, you need to follow the next steps:
1. **Select the Skill Type**: There are 4 types of Skills:
   - *Attack Skills*: Affect Attack Actions and Counters
   - *Block Skills*: Affect Block Reactions
   - *Evade Skills*: Affect Evade Reactions
   - *Stat Use Skills*: Affect Stat Challenges

2. **Select the Light Cost**: All Skills start with a cost of 1 Light. You can increase this cost to increase the **base EP** by [`Rank of the Skill`]. This increases the maximum amount of negative points the Skill can have, as well as its positive. You can increase the Light cost up to a maximum of the character’s Max Light.

3. **Buy effects**: Buy effects using the Effect Crafting rules as normal.

#### Using Skills
When you use an Action or Reaction, you can declare you are using a Skill as long as the Skill type can affect that Roll, and you immediately pay its Light Cost.

You cannot use two Skills for the same Action or Reaction. This also means that Skills can’t be combined with Applied Tools. 
A character can’t own or use Skills higher than their own Rank.

## Act 4: Equipment and Utilities
### Weapons
Designed to attack and exert force over others, weapons are the tools that determine the character’s combat capabilities.

All weapons start with an **Offensive Dice** of [`1d10+0`], this value and the capabilities of the weapon will be modified by *FOUR* choices needed to make a new weapon.

#### 1. Ranged or Melee
A weapon can only be Ranged or Melee.

Melee weapons are the most common weapon in The City. When using a Melee Weapon, you use the normal combat rules.

The alternative choice to Melee weapon is Ranged weapon. 
Ranged weapons provide the user with safety by allowing attacks from a distance, albeit expensive due to consumption of Ammunition with each shot. 
Ranged weapons will open up new tactical capabilities to the users in ways that Melee weapons can't. 
Ranged weapons have the following special rules:
- They have a base Range of 10 SQRs, which can be modified with certain Effects.
- They consume 1 Ammunition Unit per **Attack Action/Reaction**
- They don’t suffer ST DMG from losing a Clash against Block.  (CW/CL Effects apply as normal)
- They don’t suffer DMG from losing a Clash against Counter if they are out of the Range of the Countering weapon. (CW/CL Effects apply as normal)
- They don’t have a DMG Type. Instead, their DMG Type is determined by their Ammunition, allowing flexibility.
Can buy and use Special Ammunition.

***Consideration: Ammunition***
Ranged Weapons use ammunition, and so it is important to have Ammunition rules in consideration.Regular ammunition can be held freely, but specialized ammunition occupies Tool Slots. Every 5 ammunition (or fraction of 5) the character has occupies 1 Tool Slot. So, a character carrying 6 specialized ammunition has 2 Tool Slots occupied. 

Different types of ammunition can share a slot. Nothing stops a character from having one Slash Tranquilizer Bullet, two Pierce High Velocity Bullets, one Pierce Flame Bullet and one Blunt Shock Bullet, all occupying only one Slot because the total adds up to five.

If a character is out of ammo, they can still use their Ranged Weapon, but are restricted by the following:


- The Attack/Counter roll is made at disadvantage.
- Any Ranged exclusive effects do not apply.
- Any Ranged exclusive properties do not apply (except for properties that only change the roll of the attack, such as High Caliber).

#### 2. DMG Type
If you select a Melee Weapon (or if your Ranged Weapon has a fixed ammo effect such as Charge Ammo), you need to select the DMG Type of your weapon between: **Slash**, **Pierce**, or **Blunt**.
There aren’t any mechanical differences between DMG Types, but they interact with Resistances, with some characters receiving more or less DMG from certain types than others.

#### 3. Form Property
When creating a weapon, you need to select its Form Property. This represents the weapon’s physical form or what function it was made for.

Some Form Properties are exclusive to Combat Parts, a type of weapon that can be installed as part of your Augment. Further details can be found in the Augment section of the Effect List.

*Melee weapons* need to select one of the following options:

**MELEE WEAPON FORM PROPERTIES**
- **Small:**
    > `+1` Dice Max on evade

- **Medium:**
    > `+2` Dice Max

- **Long:**
    > Your Melee Attack Range is `2` Squares. On Clash Win with this weapon against an adjacent target, you may choose to move the target `1` SQR in any direction on the ground. This movement does not proc Force Damage or Opportunity Attacks. 
    > `+1` to your Throwing Range with this weapon.

- **Sturdy:**
    > `+1` Dice Max on block

- **Hybrid:**
    > You can choose to make either a Melee or Ranged Attack with this weapon, decided before the clash is rolled.
    > Incompatible Hand Properties do not apply their bonuses, and bullets are still consumed as normal for Ranged Attacks. [M] and [R] exclusive effects also do not take effect if incompatible.

- **[*COMBAT PART*] Versatile:**
    > At the start of your turn, you may change this weapon between Offensive and Defensive, maintaining Handedness.

- **[*COMBAT PART*] Innate:**
    > This Weapon may use an exclusive Skill of the Combat Part's Rank, outside of the character's normal pool of skills.

*Ranged weapons* need to select one of the following options:

**RANGED WEAPON FORM PROPERTIES**
- **Low Caliber:**
    > You don’t consume your remaining movement when making a Ranged Attack. 
    > You may make Opportunity Attacks with this weapon; your weapon is treated as having a range of `2` Squares for this purpose.

- **High Caliber:**
    > `+2` Dice Max

- **Reactive:**
    > If an ally inside the halved range of this Weapon gets attacked, you can spend a reaction to Counter it with this Weapon. This attack is incompatible with any Attack Replacement effects. 
    > On Clash Win, the attack is stopped by your clash, and damage applies as normal. On Clash Lose, the Attack repeats with the same roll, targeting the ally. The ally is still able to react to this. Clash Win/Lose effects apply as normal in both cases.

- **Hybrid:**
    > You can choose to make either a Melee or Ranged Attack with this weapon, decided before the clash is rolled. 
    > Incompatible Hand Properties do not apply their bonuses, and bullets are still consumed as normal for Ranged Attacks. [M] and [R] exclusive effects also do not take effect if incompatible.

- **[*COMBAT PART*] Recoil:**
    > After making an Attack/Counter with this weapon, you may move 2 SQR in any direction, ignoring Opportunity Attacks. On Clash Win with this weapon, you may also apply this effect as Forced Movement to the target.

- **[*COMBAT PART*] Innate:**
    > This Weapon may use an exclusive Skill of the Combat Part's Rank, outside of the character's normal pool of skills.

#### 4. Hand Property
Finally, you need to select the Hand Property of the weapon. This represents the way the weapon is wielded. Two Handed Weapons can’t be used with one hand, and One Handed Weapons don’t gain any extra benefit from being used with two hands.

Nothing stops a Sturdy weapon from taking Offensive Handed properties, or a Long weapon from taking Defensive Handed properties. Mix and match to your heart's desire! There is only one exception: 

**Ranged Weapons can only have Offensive Handed properties.**

**Hand Properties**
- **Offensive 1 Handed:**
    > `+1` Dice Power. `+1` Reaction if not Dual Wielding (does not stack)

- **Offensive 2 Handed:**
    > `+2` EP, `+2` Dice Power

- **[*Melee Only*] Defensive 1 Handed:**
    > When you Attack or Counter with this weapon and the enemy is clashing offensively, after seeing the result of the clash, you may spend a Block or Counter reaction to treat your result as a Block, still applying Weapon Clash Win and Clash Lose Effects as normal.
    > `+1` Reaction if not Dual Wielding (does not stack)

- **[*Melee Only*] Defensive 2 Handed:**
    > `+2` EP. While taking the Protect Action, after you resolve a Clash initiated by an opposing target, you may make an Attack Action with this weapon against that target for no cost. The enemy may only react with Block or Evade. This benefit may only activate once per ally you intercept a Clash for per round. The user does not count as an ally for this benefit.

***Consideration: Dual Wielding***
If you buy a One Handed weapon, you are capable of Dual Wielding it alongside another One Handed weapon. The Dual Wielding rules are copied here so you can take Dual Wielding in consideration when customizing your weapons.

Any combinations of two weapons can perform a Dual Wield attack under the following conditions:
- If you use an Action to Attack and you Clash Win against the target, you can do a second attack using the weapon that wasn’t used on the first attack. This second attack can be against any target within the weapon’s range, not necessarily the target of the first attack.
- You spend 1 Counter reaction to do this second attack, and it can only be performed once per turn.


Depending on the Hand Property of your weapons, additional benefits can apply:
- **2 Offensive**: If you Clash Win with your second attack, you restore the Counter you spent to perform it. Additionally, if you are able to make multiple Attack Actions per turn (not counting attacks made through effects such as Dual Wielding or Multi-Hit), you are not affected by the “once per turn” limit, but any additional dual-wielding attacks after the first cost 2 Counters to perform, and do not restore any reactions if successful.
- **2 Defensive**: You gain 1 extra Block Reaction.
- **1 Offensive + 1 Defensive**: You may, instead of using a Counter reaction, choose to instead use a Block reaction to perform your second attack.

#### Conclusion
A base weapon is the sum of: [Range or Melee] + [DMG Type] + [Form Property] + [Hand Property]

- Sturdy
- Defensive Two Handed



Once the base weapon has been done, you need to give your weapon a Rank and buy its Weapon Effects.

### Outfits
Designed primarily to defend, Outfit determines your Resistances and Defensive Dice.

All Outfits have a **Base Block Dice** of [`1d10+0`] and a **Base Evade Dice** of [`1d12+0`]

#### Resistances
Outfits have six Resistances, one HP Resistance and one ST Resistance, for each of the DMG Types (Slash, Pierce, Blunt). 

Resistances determine how much HP and ST Damage the character receives once they are hit. Resistances multiply the damage received depending on their level:


| Resistance Level | DMG Multiplier |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| Fatal | `x2` |
| Weak | `x1.5` |
| Normal | `x1` |
| Endured | `x0.5` |
| Ineffective | `x0.25` |
| Immune | `x0` |

All Outfits start with *Normal* resistances, and then modify resistances to fit the Resistance Levels of the Outfit’s Rank using the following table:

| Outfit Rank | HP Resistance | ST Resistance |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| -1 | `1` Fatal, `1` Weak | `2` Fatal |
| 0 | `1` Weak | `1` Fatal, `1` Weak |
| 1 | `1` Weak | `2` Weak |
| 2 | `1` Weak | `1` Weak |
| 3 | All Normal | All Normal |
| 4 | `1` Endured | All Normal |
| 5 | `1` Endured | `1` Endured |
| EX | `2` Endured | `2` Endured |

#### Resistance Trading
Resistances through Resistance Trading.

For this, you need to select two Resistances, increasing its level on one by reducing its level on the other. For example, you can select your Blunt ST Resistance and your Slash HP Resistance, both Normal, to upgrade one to Endured and lower the other one to Weak.

You may only trade a Resistance up in level to one step above the strongest Resistance available on default for the Rank of the Outfit. 

For example: if you have a Rank 3 Outfit, you may trade Resistances down to Fatal [2x], but at maximum you can only trade up to Endured [0.5x]; you cannot trade up to Ineffective [0.25x].

#### Outfit Properties
Much like weapons, outfits can also be specialized towards a certain defensive style– or none at all, if it suits the character best.
When Creating an Outfit, you must select one of the three following Outfit Properties which can characterize a specialization between Blocking, Evading, or neither.

- **Armored:**
    > `+1` to Block Dice Power. On Clash Win with Block, deal half of the roll OR the difference between rolls as Stagger Damage to the opponent, whichever is greater.

- **Swift:**
    > `+1` to Evade Dice Power. On Clash Win with Evade, your Recycled Evade penalty only increments in values of `-1`, instead of `-2`.

- **Balanced:**
    > `+2` EP, `+1` Maximum Light for the Character

#### Conclusion
Once you finish Resistance Trading and selecting the Outfit Property, you can start to modify your Outfit Effects, following the EP amount based on the Outfit Rank and Property.

### Tools
Tools allow the character to do specialized work on different fields, be it combat or narrative encounters.

#### Tool Classification
Tools are divided into how much EP and usability they have:
- **Consumable Tools** can only be used once. In exchange, they have a lower price than the reusable kind.
- **Reusable Tools** have three uses per day but are more expensive.
- **Handless Tools** can be used with little effort, and thus have lower EP than their other counterparts.
- **1-Handed Tools** require a free hand to use, and have a base amount of EP.
- **2-Handed Tools** require both hands of the user to be operated properly, but in return have a higher amount of EP than other options.

When you buy a Tool, you also need to select its type as well. Both types of tools use the [Skill] table for effects.
- **Standalone Tools** can be used with an Action and a Stat Challenge, stat chosen on Tool creation. They can be used on either the user or an adjacent target.
  - If the target is the user or a willing target, roll a Challenge Roll with a negative modifier equal to the Rank of the tool.
  - If they are an unwilling target, make a Resisted Roll with a negative modifier equal to the same Stat of the target as was used for the roll.
  - On Success or Partial Success, all positive effects of the tool activate as normal on the specified target.
  - On Failure, all negative effects of the tool activate instead on the user (or a specified target, if applicable) and no use is consumed.
- **Applied Tools** act as 0-Cost Skills of the Tool's EP, and function on top of a Weapon or Outfit’s effects when used, with the applicable equipment type chosen on Tool creation. These tools are classified as Skills when activated-- thus they cannot be stacked with normal Skills. 
  - A character may use a Weapon Applied Tool without an Action if they are holding it and have the weapon that they are applying it to, in which they declare use when Attacking or Countering with that weapon. Otherwise, they may use an Action to apply it to a weapon and immediately swap into that weapon if not already being held, in which it will activate on the next use of that weapon.
  - Outfit Applied Tools may be used with no extra Action as long as the tool is already being held, and only need to be declared when using a Block or Evade reaction.
- **Market Tools** are tools that don’t follow conventional rules and instead have their own specified uses. The Item List has a big list of examples, and your GM is encouraged to create custom tools following this logic for their own campaigns.

### Augments
Augments represent different passive improvements of the character, always present on them no matter their Weapons or Outfits.

#### Mechanic and Narrative
It is important to emphasize that, in the world of Project Moon, augments can have a plethora of forms. Cybernetic, biogenetic prosthesis, drugs, surgeries, mutations, even training and instincts are all valid as flavors for Augments.

Talk with your GM about the narrative nature of your Augment. In general a fully human character doesn’t need to feel boxed into something specific just to use Augment effects.

#### Augment Rank and Upgrading
The Augment Rank increases *automatically* with the Rank of the character. A Rank 1 Character that reaches Rank 2 will immediately upgrade their augment to Rank 2.

Augment Rank only increases the amount of EP that the Augment has, and *doesn’t confer **any** Effects by itself*. The character needs to buy Augment Effects *individually* once points are available.

For example, a fully augmented character at Rank 1 will have 8 Points in Positive Effects and 4 Points in Negative Effects (filling the maximum of 4 EP at base).

When the character Rank increases to Rank 2, their Augment Base EP increases to 8, allowing for a maximum of 16 Points in Positive Effects if the character also has 8 Points in Negative Effects.

After a character increases in Rank they need to buy effects to fill these new spaces. While 4 of these new Points will already be open to add Positive Effects, they will need to buy additional Negative Effects to make use of the remaining capacity granted by the Rank.

## Act 5: Core Systems
Once you have a Character, the most common questions are “*What do I do*?” and “*How well did I do it?*”

To solve these questions, the system uses the PbtA system of rolling 2d6s in a scale to determine the results.

### Rolling
#### Base Roll: Challenge Roll
The most basic roll in the game is called Challenge Roll.

For doing a Challenge Roll, you roll two six-faced dice (2d6) and add up their results.

> 2d6 rolled = one rolls 6 and the other 3 = final result is 9 [6+3]

Normally, Challenge Rolls ask you to add a Stat to the roll, in which you add the Stat value to the final result.

> If the previous roll was a Fortitude Challenge (character has 2 Fortitude), the final result would be 11 [9+2]

Finally, Challenge Roll can have modifiers, positive or negative, that are also added to the final result.

> If the previous roll had a Modifier of -3, the final result would be 8 [11-3]

The final result is then compared with the next table that determines the outcome:

| Final Result | Outcome | Description |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| 6 or less | Failure | The action fails and negative consequences occur as a result. |
| 7, 8, or 9 | Partial Success | The action is completed, but there is a cost or a new problem arising from the solution. |
| 10 or more | Complete Success | The action is completed without problems or extra costs. |

For example, assuming the character wanted to kick down a door, the results could be:

| Outcome | Description |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| Failure | The door is still intact and the character sprains their ankle |
| Partial Success | The door is kicked down but only partially, needing more time to kick it down completely or needing to enter through slowly |
| Complete Success | The door is kicked down with no problem, leaving a wide space to run through |

#### Difficulty Modifiers
These rolls are normally modified by the circumstances in which they are done. Kicking down a wooden door in an abandoned house is different to trying to kick down the metal door of a corporate facility.

This is normally represented through Difficulty Modifiers, that change the roll Final Result. They follow the next scale:

| Difficulty | Modifier | Description |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| Very Easy | `+2` | One should question why are you making them roll in the first place. |
| Easy | `+1` | Actions that even untrained people can do, like climbing a tree with multiple handholds or recalling a famous figure. |
| Normal | `0` | This is the average roll in most situations. |
| Moderate | `-1` | Actions that represent the character needing some talent, like climbing a very tall building or deciphering an old code. |
| Hard | `-2` | Actions that represent some kind of training, like doing scientific research or climbing a slippery surface. |
| Very Hard | `-3` | Actions that are difficult even to the trained, like recalling an Association secret or climbing a perfectly vertical slippery surface. |
| Extreme | `-4` | Actions that seem impossible for the average person. |
| Mastery | `-5` | Actions that need an exceptional level of mastery. |

#### Advantage and Disadvantage
External factors that don’t correlate directly to the task at hand, like extra tools to help with or bad weather, can give the character Advantage or Disadvantage depending on the task. This represents the act of rolling two times in a row, keeping only one of the Final Results.

If you roll with Advantage, you roll the Challenge Roll two times, and you keep *the highest* result.

If you roll with Disadvantage, you roll the Challenge Roll two times, and you keep *the lowest* result.

Advantage and Disadvantage cancel each other out. Unless explicitly stated, Advantage and Disadvantage *aren’t* cumulative, you only ever roll two times, no matter the number of sources of Advantage or Disadvantage you have.

#### Assisting
While solidarity is uncommon to see in the City, it is not unusual for PCs and other allies to help each other overcome difficult tasks. This is counted as Assisting.

Any character can Assist others. The character *receiving* the Assist is considered as the actor of the Main Roll.

When assisting out of combat, a character must pay 1 Light and **select one** of the following options:
- Give Advantage to the roll [this Advantage is cumulative with other sources]
- Give a bonus to the roll equal to the character's own Stat. For example, while Assisting a Fortitude Roll, the character can give his Fortitude as a bonus to the main roll.

The GM determines how long the Assist action takes, the range at which it can be performed, and how many characters can Assist the same target. If insignificant justification is given for how a character should be able to Assist, it can be denied for no cost to the players.

#### Opposed Rolls
In some situations, you will be competing with another person in something not directly related to combat, like an arm-wrestling contest. 

In this case, your GM should select between two options depending on how they want to approach the situation.

The first option is to do an Opposed Roll: this means that both you and your opponent perform a Challenge Roll and instead of following the Challenge Roll table, the highest result obtains a “Complete Success” while the lower result obtains a “Failure”.

This option is encouraged when both characters are doing something active, like, both being part of the same chase or both trying to convince the same audience.

The second option is to do a Resisted Roll, this means that only one side of the conflict rolls, with a negative Modifier equal to the other target’s Rank or appropriate Stat, depending on the GM, then following the normal results of the Challenge Rolls.

### Character Progression
As characters complete contracts, jobs and adventures they learn about the world and improve their capabilities.

#### Experience Points
Characters accumulate Experience Points by doing the next actions:
- *Presence*: At the end of a session, give 1 EXP to all players.
- *Learning by Failure*: If in this session three “Failures” were achieved by the team, give 1 EXP to all players.
- *Combat*: If this session they have a combat, give the EXP to all players depending on the combat:
  - *Easy*: A combat that is almost resolved by itself and without any big cost in resources doesn't give any experience.
  - *Normal*: A combat that costs something to the players, by HP or Light, gives 1 experience.
  - *Hard*: A combat that almost mutilates at least one character gives 2 experience.
  - *Very Hard*: A combat that almost or successfully mutilates one or more characters gives 4 experience.
- *Overcoming difficulties*: Give between 2 and 4 EXP based on the difficulty or importance of the obstacle/problem dealt with.
- *Other rewards*: As a GM, you are encouraged to create your own Experience Rewards! Think about your PCs themes and support them via progression.

#### Leveling Up
For every `8` EXP points accumulated, the character increases their level by 1.

Each time a character levels up, they receive the following benefits:
- Can increase two Stats by `+1` or one Stat by `+2`, within Rank limits.
- Can move one Stat point to a different Stat, under the same limits of [`Rank+2`] for maximum and `-1` for minimum.
- Can choose to perform any of the following actions **ONCE** each.
  - Learn a new Skill of [`Character Rank`] Rank.
    - Note: A character can only have a maximum of `6` Skills. If this option would exceed the amount of skills a character has, you are allowed to treat it as an additional [Change Skill] option.
  - Change known Skill effects. If the Skill is of lower Rank than the Character, it also increases it to [`Character Rank`] Rank.
  - Forget a Skill. This removes the Skill completely unless replaced by a new added Skill.

#### Ranking Up
Every 3 Level Ups [Levels 3, 6, 9, 12 and 15], the character Ranks Up.

When Ranking up, increase your Rank *first*, and then proceed with Leveling Up. For example, when the character reaches Level 3 [the transition from Rank 1 to Rank 2], your limit on Stats is 4 [Rank 2 + 2] and any Skill you Learn or Change can be Rank 2.

When Ranking Up, your character's Derived Attributes will change as well, so remember to change them accordingly. Official character sheets may calculate this automatically, but you can always double-check with the formulas provided earlier.

Additionally, you now have the Action Economy of your new Rank.

## Act 6: Combat
Combat is the foundational pillar of the PM TTRPG, as most conflicts in The City are solved through the clash of weapons. 

### Timing
Combat is divided into Rounds that are made up of different Turns.

Each Round is, approximately, `5` seconds inside the game. GMs are encouraged to change this as is convenient to their narrative and maps, but never quicker than `3` seconds and never longer than `10` seconds.

Turns are taken in order from the character with the Highest Speed through to the character with the Lowest Speed. 

When all characters involved in combat finish their Turns, the Round Ends and a new Round Starts.

### Combat Start
When combat is about to start, It is important to have four things clear:

#### Intent - What is the objective?
Not only should players be informed of the objective of the fight, but the GM should inform characters, to the best of the character abilities, the intention of the enemies they are confronting. Is this a fight to the death? To capture? Maybe they want something and will ignore the opponents if an opportunity to take it appears?

#### Surprise - Was anyone caught off-guard?
Most people in The City expect combat, but surprise attacks still happen for those walking into the wrong place at the wrong time.

If any side has started with the conditions to Surprise the other, a “Round 0” begins only for them, where each member of the side that engaged in the Surprise can make one One-Sided Attack Action before combat properly begins.

#### Speed - Who goes first?
When Surprise has been resolved, all Characters involved in combat roll [`1d6+Justice`] to determine their Speed, ordered from highest Speed to lowest Speed for turn order.

In case of Ties, PCs go before NPCs, GM resolves ties between NPCs, players resolve ties between PCs.

#### Backup - Joining mid-combat
If a character wants to join the combat when it has already started, the GM puts them on what they consider the area limits of the combat and the character rolls Speed at the end of the appropriate Round, participating as normal in the next Round.

### Combat Actions
#### Action Economy
Action Economy in this game is flexible, depending on Rank and even certain effects that give the characters multiple uses of their Action Economy. There are three resources used in the Action Economy:
- **Movement**: This action represents the character's capacity to position themselves in a tactical manner during combat.
- **Action**: These actions represent active decisions the character can make during their turn.
  - All characters have Actions determined by their Rank following the table below.
- **Reaction**: These actions represent reactive decisions the character can make when targeted by something or someone else.
  - All characters have Reactions determined by their Rank following the table below.
  - Characters can earn extra Reactions thanks to Outfit and Augment effects.
  - Characters can earn extra Reactions thanks to their equipment like sturdy, small or one-handed weapons.

**Available Actions and Reactions to Rank**
| Rank | Actions | Reactions |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| 0 | `1` | `0` |
| 1 | `1` | `1` |
| 2 | `1` | `2` |
| 3 | `2` | `3` |
| 4 | `2` | `4` |
| 5 | `3` | `5` |
| EX | `3` | `6` |

***Action Economy Refreshing***
At the start of a character’s turn, they recover all Actions, Reactions and Movement expended before that point. If something wasn’t expended, it isn’t recovered.

#### Movement
Movement can only be used during the Character’s Turns. Unless altered for some effect, all Characters have only one Movement Action: Tactical Movement

**Tactical Movement:**

You can move up to [`3 + [Justice/2] Rounded Down`] SQRs in any direction that isn’t interrupted by obstacles.

Movement can be divided during a turn; you can move `2` SQRs > do an Action > move the remaining `1` SQRs.

If you have Haste, you increase your SQRs by your Haste stacks during that Turn.
If you have Bind, you decrease your SQRs by your Bind stacks during that Turn.

**Consideration: Opportunity Attacks**

If you move out of the Melee Range of a Character, they can use their Reaction to do an Opportunity Attack against you. 

You can choose to use a Reaction to Block or Evade and proc a Clash, but if you Lose this Clash your Movement Action is immediately expended, and you can’t keep moving.

You can choose to take the Opportunity Attack one-sided and not consume a Reaction, in which you take the attack normally, but are free to move after the attack is resolved (unless the attack Staggers you).

In the case of Opportunity Attacks that are made up of multiple attacks, such as through Multi-Hit or similar effects, the Clash risking movement only applies to the first attack. Similarly, Opportunity Attacks cannot proc Dual Wielding as they are Reactions.

**Consideration: Difficult Terrain**

Be it ruins, rubble or even someone throwing marbles on the ground, walking through Difficult Terrain increases the amount of movement SQRs needed to move through a tile. As a general rule, most Difficult Terrain consume `2` SQRs instead of `1` when moving through them.

Difficult Terrain is additive with other movement limiting effects. So, if someone already is consuming 2 SQR of movement for other reasons and tries to walk on Difficult Terrain, they consume only 3 SQRs of movement per SQR, not 4.

**Consideration: Hazards**

Hazards generally inflict Status Effects when walking through them or ending a Turn on them but can impose other disadvantages as appropriate. Some hazards may scale in severity depending on the effect, up to GM discretion. Examples include:
- **Exposed Fire**: Take `1d6` Burn.
- **Broken Glass**: Take `1d6` Bleed.
- **Exhaust Fumes**: Take `1d6` Smoke.
- **Chilling Frost**: Take `1d6` Frostbite.
- **Tear Gas**: While inside the hazard, if a character does not have Smoke Immunity or other forms of Smoke Reduction, their reactions are made with a penalty of `-X`.
- **Thick Fog**: While inside the hazard, if a character does not have Smoke Immunity or a proper item to see through the hazard, their Insight rolls are made at disadvantage.
- **Cleansing Gas**: While moving through the hazard, a character must make a Fortitude roll with a modifier of `-X`. On Failure/Partial Success,  the character takes `4d6`/`2d6` Stagger Damage. Characters Staggered within the hazard remain Staggered for `1` extra Round.
- **NOTE**: Any hazard that applies a status will only trigger once per SQR of said hazard during a single character’s movement action. Moving back through already travelled SQRs should not reapply further hazards, but may be up to GM discretion.

**Falling Damage**

Whether brawling at the edge of a rooftop, or traveling treacherous cliffs out in the Outskirts, sometimes characters can take a bit of a tumble. Characters can generally withstand falls from greater heights the higher the Rank they are. When calculating fall damage from terrain or other effects (such as being thrown), the following formula can be used as a baseline.

> `(Squares Fallen - [Character Rank+1])d8 HP DMG`

So, for example: A Rank 2 Character can fall up to 3 SQR worth of distance before taking damage, but every square past that will be an additional 1d8 worth of HP DMG.

**Forced Movement**

Sometimes during combat, it is possible for characters to get thrown and tossed around rather violently. This movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks, but does interact with Hazards.

In cases of Forced Movement that pushes a character into a hard surface or other appropriate forces, the following formula can be used as a baseline. This formula also applies when effects specify that Force Damage can occur.

> `(Squares of Unspent Forced Movement)d8 HP DMG`

#### Actions
Actions can only be used during the Character’s Turn.

All Characters have the following Actions:
- Melee Attack
- Ranged Attack
- Assist
- Change Weapons
- Change Outfit
- Convert to Reactions
- Dash
- Delay
- Disengage
- Grapple
  - Break Free (when Grappled)
  - Throw (when Grappling)
- Protect
- Ready
- Reduce Status
- Take Cover
- Use Tool

**Melee Attack**

Your character spends an action to make a Melee Attack against a Target in their Melee Range (`1` SQR unless stated otherwise) using one of their Melee Weapons.

If the target reacts, a Clash occurs; if not, the attack is One-Sided. In both cases, you use the weapon’s Offensive Dice.

Counters against you that are outside of the *Countering* weapon range, like someone with a non-long weapon countering your long weapon at maximum range, don’t deal HP DMG or ST DMG against you if they succeed.

**Consideration: Throwing Weapons**

You can throw Melee Weapons up to your [`Fortitude+Rank`] SQRs using the Ranged Attack Action.

When doing this, you have a modifier of `-2` to your attack roll, and the roll is done with Disadvantage. The weapon drops on the adjacent SQR of the target closest to you.

**Ranged Attack**
Your character makes an attack against a target within the Effective Range (`10` SQR unless stated otherwise) of one of their Ranged Weapons, using all their remaining movement, if any.

If the target reacts, a Clash occurs; if not, the attack is One-Sided. In both cases, you use the weapon’s Offensive Dice.

Counters against you that are outside of the *Countering* weapon range, like someone with a Melee Weapon countering your Ranged Attack at maximum range, don’t deal HP DMG or ST DMG against you if they succeed.

Clash Lose against Block using a Ranged Attack doesn't deal ST DMG to you.

**Consideration: Non-Lethal Attacks**

There are moments where you don’t want to kill your target. When you do a Melee Attack or Ranged Attack Action, and even when you do a Counter or Opportunity Attack Reaction, you can choose to do a non-lethal attack.

You roll your Attack with Disadvantage, if your attack reduces your target to `0` HP, they fall unconscious and *alive* with `1` HP instead.

If the target is staggered, you don’t suffer Disadvantage on the roll.

**Consideration: Fighting Without Weapons or Outfits**

It is rare, but possible, that a character is found unarmed or without a combat outfit, using normal non-reinforced clothes.

When unarmed, you can’t use the Ranged Attack Action. You can perform the Melee Attack Action, but your attack is done with [`1d8+Fortitude`], with no bonus of `Rank`, only benefiting from the Clash Lose and Clash Win effects of your Augment. This attack type can be Blunt, Slash or Pierce, and can be considered nonlethal without disadvantage. This is considered an Improvised Attack. 

A character can choose to perform an Improvised Attack instead of a regular Attack Action or Counter Reaction even if they have weapons drawn.

When you aren’t using an Outfit, all your Resistances are Fatal and all your Defensive Dices [Block and Evade] are set to `1d8+0` base, modified by your Block and Evade modifiers as normal.

**Assist**

As per the rules for Assisting out of combat, Assisting during combat works similarly. In combat, a character must pay 1 Light to provide the recipient Advantage on any one roll, expiring at the end of their next turn if not utilized first. 

The GM determines how long the Assist action takes, the range at which it can be performed, and how many characters can Assist the same target.

**Change Weapons**

Your character can switch the weapons they are holding with those in their inventory. To use a weapon, it must be unsheathed and held in the appropriate number of hands.
Once per turn, your character can sheathe or unsheathe one One-Handed Weapon or Tool for free.

If you sheath a weapon or tool and immediately unsheathe another, it uses an Action, except for the free option for One-Handed weapons or tools. When swapping weapons, your character must have free hands. 
For example, you can’t switch from dual-wielding two One-Handed Weapons to a Two-Handed Tool without first sheathing one weapon for free. Dropping a weapon or tool on the ground is always free, but picking one up from the ground uses an Action. 

Reactions are calculated based on your currently held weapons– for example; sheathing a Sturdy weapon will mean you lose the applicable Block Reaction, but unsheathing a Small weapon will gain you the applicable Counter Reaction. 
Weapons only grant their Reactions the first time they are swapped to in a Round.

**Change Outfit**

Your character can change their equipped Outfit with one in their inventory, taking on the new effects and resistances of that Outfit. This does not retrigger any “Combat Start” effects. When taking this Action, the user consumes all of their Reactions.

**Convert to Reactions**

Your character prepares to react to their opponents’ movements, instead of taking active action.

You can transform Actions into Universal Reactions at the end of your turn in a ratio of `1:1`. However, remember that your action economy *resets* at the start of your next Turn, so any Reactions unused are lost at the start of your next Turn.

This action doesn’t activate any Status Effect that normally activates with Action use, like Bleed.

**Dash**

Your character adds [`3+Justice`] SQRs of movement to their Movement Action. If the Movement Action was expended (for example, by doing a Ranged Attack) this Action doesn't have any value.

This action doesn’t activate any Status Effect that normally activates with Action use, like Bleed.

**Delay**

Your character waits for others to act first.

You can Delay your *entire turn* (this includes the Movement Action) after anyone else that comes after you in the turn order. This affects when you recover your Reactions, and sets your initiative to the new value. If you decide to Delay, you maintain your existing Reactions (or lack thereof) until the delayed turn occurs.

You can Delay into the next round, starting at an initiative equal to the maximum value of your speed roll. If you do so, this delayed turn replaces your turn for that round.

**Disengage**

Your character can move freely, being incapable of being targeted by Opportunity Attacks for the rest of the turn.

This action doesn’t activate any Status Effect that normally activates with Action use, like Bleed.

**Grapple**

Your character lunges at an adjacent target, starting an Opposed Challenge Roll. If the target isn’t Staggered, you roll Fortitude with Disadvantage, while the target can respond with Fortitude or Justice without using a Reaction. Winning this roll initiates a Grapple Condition, immobilizing the target and limiting their responses.

When you successfully use the Grappling Action, both you and your target enter the Grappling Condition, which has two roles: Grappler and Target.

- **Grappling Condition (Grappler):**
  - At the start of your turn, use one Universal Reaction to maintain the Grapple.
  - While maintaining the Grapple:
    > You can move yourself and your target, but each square (SQR) of movement counts as two. Without Haste or other modifiers, you can move up to `1` SQR with your target.
    > You can spend Actions to reduce your target’s Reactions on a `1:1` ratio. 
    > You may also use the Throwing Action and end the Grapple.
  - If you become Staggered, the Grapple automatically ends.
- **Grappling Condition (Target):**
  - You lose your Movement Action.
  - You lose access to all actions and reactions. 
  - You lose access to the Opportunity Attack Reaction.
  - When attacked, you can use your Reaction to try and Break Free.
  - You cannot be Grappled by more than one Grappler at a time.

**Break Free (Grappled)**

When you are Grappled, you can try to Break Free as an Action on your turn or as a Reaction according to the Grappled Condition rules.
- This requires a Resisted Fortitude or Justice Challenge Roll, with a penalty equal to `Grappler’s Fortitude`.
- Any type of success (Complete or Partial) breaks the Grapple.
- If you fail, you get a cumulative `+1` bonus for your next attempt until you succeed.
Other characters can also use the Break Free Action to end a Grapple, but they do not get the cumulative `+1` bonus for failure.
After successfully breaking free, you cannot be Grappled again until the end of your next turn.

**Throw (Grappling)**

As the Grappler, you can use an Action to throw your target.
- You can throw the target `([(Fortitude + Rank) / 2, rounded down] + 1)` SQRs in any direction, including up and down, for appropriate Falling Damage or Force Damage as applicable.
- This forced movement does not provoke Opportunity Attacks.
- The Grapple ends once the target is thrown.
You can use this Action for free if you do it in the same turn you start the Grapple, without taking any other Actions beforehand. A successfully thrown target cannot be Grappled again until the end of their next turn or the end of the grappler's next turn, whichever is later.

**Protect**

Your character can use Reactions to defend against attacks on adjacent squares as if they were being attacked. This includes defending against Opportunity Attacks on allies as long as they are still adjacent to you when moving. When protecting, you can only use Defensive Reactions (Evade or Block) to intercept. You can use the Opportunity Attack reaction as normal. This protection lasts until your next turn.
  - You can use this action at any time to enter a protecting state

**Ready**

You can Ready an Action on your turn, forfeiting one Reaction to do so.

Instead of occurring when this Action is used, the readied Action will occur upon a condition declared by the user based upon perceivable circumstances- at the latest, at the start of the character’s next turn; though this does not consume any of the Actions for that next turn. GM discretion can be used to ensure the circumstances of a Readied Action are feasible and sensible for the situation.

If an Attack Action is being readied, it is not eligible for dual-wielding. 

**Reduce Status**

Your character reduces negative Status Effects on themself or an adjacent and willing target. You can reduce a total of `[(Prudence or Temperance)+Rank]*2` Stacks of Status Effects (this is applied individually across all statuses, not the full count per status). 

Only Burn, Frostbite, Bleed, Tremor, Rupture, Sinking, Thorns, and Smoke can be reduced by this Action, but GMs are encouraged to expand this list if homebrew effects that deal direct or indirect DMG to the characters are involved.
Effects that “pause” or otherwise stop natural reduction of a status, such as Renewed Blaze, Hemorrhage, and Deep Chill, can be reduced at a rate of [`(Prudence or Temperance)/2`], but no other status effects can be reduced alongside them.

This action doesn’t activate any Status Effect that normally activates with Action use, like Bleed.

**Take Cover**

Your character takes a defensive position behind a larger object, gaining the benefits of Cover.

There are two types of cover: 
- **Half Cover**: Grants `Rank` Power to Defensive Dice against Ranged Attacks. This normally means that at least `25%` of the body of the character is obscured by cover.
- **Full Cover**: Makes a character untargetable by Ranged attacks. This normally means that the entire body of the character is obscured by cover.
Cover modifiers only apply if the Cover is between the character and the opponent.

**Use Tool**

Your character uses a Tool using the rules of Tool usage depending on if it is a Standalone, Applied or Market Tool. Remember that Applied Tools can be used without an Action if they are already being wielded alongside the appropriate equipment.

#### Reactions
Reactions can be used at any moment of the round as long as the *Character fulfills the requisites to use that Reaction*.

A Character can always choose to *NOT* use a Reaction, even if the situation prompts it.

Additionally, certain equipment can provide specialized types of Reactions. If an additional Reaction is granted as the result of an effect or property and isn’t already specified as one of the following types, unspecified Reactions are considered Universal Reactions and can be used for all the options present here.

All Characters have the following Reactions:
- **Block:**
    > You can use this Reaction when you are the target of a Melee Attack or Ranged Attack Action.
    > You engage, provoking a Clash against the Attack using the Block Dice of your Outfit.
    > On Clash Win, you negate the attack and do ST DMG to the attacker equal to `[Block Roll-Attack Roll]`.
    > On Clash Lose, you reduce the final result of the Attacker by `Block Roll`.
- **Counter:**
    > You can use this Reaction when you are the target of a Melee Attack or Ranged Attack Action.
    > You engage, provoking a Clash against the Attack using the Offensive Dice of one of your Weapons. 
    > On Clash Win, you negate the attack and, if the target is in the effective Range of your weapon, your opponent receives your attack as normal. There are no benefits on Clash Lose.
- **Evade:**
    > You can use this Reaction when you are the target of a Melee Attack or Ranged Attack Action.
    > You engage, provoking a Clash against the Attack using the Evade Dice of your Outfit.
    > On Clash Win, you negate the attack and your Character recovers ST equal to `Evade Roll`. You also gain a Recycled Evade.
    > If you use a Recycled Evade, you suffer a penalty of `-2` to Power. Each time you win a Clash with a Recycled Evade, you increase that penalty by an additional `-2`, cumulative with each reuse. Recycled Evades do not apply the Effects of Skills used on the original Evade. Using a skill with a Recycled Evade costs the appropriate Light as normal.
    > If you Clash Lose with a Recycled Evade, use a different Reaction that isn’t Evading (including using a separate Reaction to Evade again) or you start a new Turn, you lose the Recycled Evade and its associated penalty. 
- **Opportunity Attack:**
    > You can use this Reaction when someone is moving out of the Melee Range of one of your weapons without using the Disengage Action.
    > You spend a Reaction to perform an Attack Action with the weapon that the target is leaving the range of, while the Target can select to use the Block or Evade Reaction to defend themselves, or otherwise take the hit One-Sided.
    > If the target defends and their result is Clash Lose, their Movement Action is expended, and they don’t leave your Melee Range.
    > If the target defends and their result is Clash Win, or if they decide to take the attack One-Sided, they can keep moving.

#### Clashes
When two *Combat Dice* [that means, Offensive and Defensive Dices, not Challenge Rolls] engage, this is a Clash. The higher result is considered a Clash Win and the lower result is considered a Clash Lose. If both results are the same, this becomes a Tie. On Ties, both parties reroll their dice as normal, applying any Skills or additional effects without spending additional resources.

If a character receives a One-Sided Attack (where only the attacker rolls, whether by the defender’s choice or through lack of reactions/etc.), **the Clash Win effects of the Attacker are activated as if they had the Higher Result. **

For example, if someone had Inflict Burn on Clash Win, they would inflict Burn on One-Sided Attacks.

### Damage and Injury
#### Damage Calculations

When a Melee Attack or Ranged Attack Action hits the target, they start the Damage Calculation, using the Final Result of the Attack and the Resistances of the Outfit:

**HP DMG**
`([Final Result] x [Outfit HP Resistance of DMG Type]) + After Resistance HP DMG`

**ST DMG**
`([Final Result] x [Outfit ST Resistance of DMG Type]) + After Resistance ST DMG`

HP DMG and ST DMG are calculated separately. Once calculated, reduce your HP or ST equal to the calculated damage. The minimum ST and HP you can have is 0, if an attack would drop a character’s values below that, they stop at 0.

Effects that depend on the Clash Result, such as Regen, occur *after* Damage Calculation, Effects that affect DMG Calculation, like Bursting Rupture or Tremor, are applied in Damage Calculation.

#### Staggered and Stagger Threshold

A character normally takes ST DMG during combat, stunning and exposing them.

When a Character reaches 0 in their Stagger Threshold they are ***Staggered* until the *end* of the *Next* Round**. This applies no matter how early into the Current Round the character becomes staggered. 

While under the effect of the Staggered Condition, the character reduces all their Outfit Resistances to *Fatal* [`x2`] and they reduce all their Movement, Actions and Reactions to `0` until they start a round without the Staggered Condition.

When a character is Staggered, they suffer `5` SP DMG and recover `1` Light.

When the end of the Next Round is reached and the round after that begins, the Staggered character immediately recovers from stagger, regains their reactions, and continues through the regular turn order. They also gain an additional `+10` Stagger over their original threshold, which goes away at the end of combat if any remains.

Additionally, Staggered characters are easier to Grapple, and the GM should consider that they are more exposed in general for other kinds of maneuvers as applicable.

#### Bleed Out, Scarred Condition and Dying

While a grave wound may render a character unable to continue fighting, death is not so quick to claim them. When a character reaches `0` HP, they are considered out of combat–  they clear all statuses on themself and enter a Bleed Out state.

**Bleed Out**

Upon entering a Bleed Out state, the character has `3` rounds, starting with the next round after entering the state, to be stabilized (progressing at the end of the round). There following actions stabilize characters in Bleed Out:
- Reaching the end of combat.
- Healing to the character in a Bleed Out, be it with a Medkit or otherwise. Out of combat healing cannot be used for this purpose. The character performing the healing must be adjacent to the character in the Bleed Out state.
- Performing a Prudence check on the target, requiring a Success. The character performing the Prudence check must be adjacent to the character in the Bleed Out state.


A stabilized character remains out of combat. If they are healed to `25%` of their max HP, they may get up and re-enter combat, regaining full stagger and rejoining the turn order with their original initiative.
At the end of combat, if a character was stabilized through methods other than healing, they return to having `25%` of their maximum HP.
If the character is not stabilized by the end of the `3rd` round, they become Scarred.

**Scarred**

When a character becomes Scarred, this means they cannot afford to suffer additional stress to their body. If a Scarred character would become Scarred again, they instead die immediately.

A character can’t normally recover from being Scarred, but if they buy a Backup Body, this new body starts unscarred, even if it is modeled after the Scarred person.

**Brutality**

For especially cruel conflicts, combatants may wish to ensure that a downed character is properly finished off. 

Using this rule, characters that are in Bleed Out are not considered out of combat until stabilized, and can be attacked as normal. An attack against a character in Bleed Out automatically succeeds unless intercepted, and reduces the timer on their Bleed Out by one round.

**Sacrificial Injury (Limb Loss)**

Even those brought to the brink will sometimes have the strength to push forward– but not without sacrifice. 

A character may elect, upon taking damage that would reduce their HP to `0`, to nullify that damage (and if this was during a clash, any effects taken) entirely, at the cost of losing one of their limbs in the process. The limb is chosen by the user, between one of their arms or legs.

Upon experiencing Limb Loss, you suffer Disadvantage to all Challenges that require the use of that limb to be performed properly. Recovering a Limb costs `200k ₳`.

If you lose an arm, you lose the capacity to use that hand. If you lose a leg, you reduce your base movement by `50% rounded down` SQRs. A character suffering arm Limb Loss may wield a two-handed weapon with one hand, but they suffer Disadvantage while doing so.

A character without arms can’t use weapons or tools, a character without legs can only move with the Dash Action unless they have Haste or similar effects.

### Panic and Sanity

PCs are exposed to SP DMG by the different sources of stress they experience.

Characters can lose SP from:
- **Pressure**: When entering lethal combat against opponents of higher Rank, you take `5` SP DMG for each Rank of difference between you and the highest Rank enemy.
- **Getting Staggered**: When your ST is reduced to `0`, you take `5` SP DMG. 
- **Bleed Out (Ally)**: When one of your allies has their HP reduced to `0`, you take `5` SP DMG for each Rank of difference between you and your ally, with their Rank being treated as `1` higher for this purpose.
- **Pushing Limits**: Up to Prudence times per combat, if you do not have the requisite Light to use a Skill, you may consume SP at a rate of `5` per Light replaced in order to use the skill. You may consume SP even if this would push you into Panic, but only to the next Light that would be available. 
(ex: if you have 8 SP, this may be used for at most 2 Light)
- **Other Stressors**: Within GM discretion, certain environments and encounters can cause mental harm even outside of combat. Don’t be afraid to mess around with Sanity damage if characters seem too comfortable.

Characters can gain SP from:
- **Enemy Takedown**: When downing an enemy, you regain `3` SP.
- **Combat End**: When ending combat, you regain `5+Prudence` SP.
- **Unpanicking**: When coming out of a Panic state, you regain `15` SP.
If this was caused due to an “immediate unpanic”, as detailed in the Panic types, regain only `10` SP.
- **Unpanicking an Ally**: When succeeding on a check to Unpanic an Ally, you regain `3` SP for each Rank of difference between you and your ally, with their Rank being treated as `1` higher for this purpose.

For any Rank difference calculations, assume any differences that would result in a negative number will instead just have no effect; ie: you do not gain Sanity from a lower Rank ally going into Bleed Out.

When a Character hits `0` SP, they begin to Panic. Unless caused by the Sinking status effect, they roll a `1d4`, and the Panic type matching the result of that roll is applied to them. 

While Panicking, a character cannot use Skills. Other forms of SP regen/gain will not bring them out of Panic, except for Combat End.

Characters can be unpanicked via another character making a Prudence Challenge Roll with a penalty of the panicking Character’s Rank, taking them out of Panic on a Success. Partial Success does not unpanic the character, but does grant a `+1` to all following attempts, additive with any other Partial Successes.
If the panicked target reaches an amount of negative SP equal to 50% their max SP or the Goading Strike skill effect is applied, this Panic is enhanced, allowing the user to control the behavior of the target. The unit who caused the SP loss or used the skill will be referred to as the ‘Provoker’ for this table.

| 1d4 Result | Panic Behavior | Goaded Panic Behavior |
| ----------- | ----------- | ----------- |
| 1 (Fight) | On their turn, the Character must move adjacent to the nearest Target and use their Action(s) to Attack the nearest available Target, including allies. If there are multiple Targets the same distance away from the Character, the Target is chosen at random. When this Character takes down a Target, immediately unpanic. | The Character can be forced to Attack a specific Target by the Provoker. |
| 2 (Flight) | On their turn, the Character must use the Disengage Action and move as far away as they can from any enemies. If the furthest they can move takes them through a hazard, they must move through it. If they have any additional Actions, they must use them to Dash and increase their movement. When this Character ends their turn `9` SQRs away from any other Targets (including allies), immediately unpanic. | The Character can be forced to move in a specific direction by the Provoker. This doesn’t specify complicated directions, but simple linear orders like ‘go north’, ‘go to that wall’, or ‘go away’. |
| 3 (Fawn) | On their turn, the Character must move adjacent to the nearest Target (chosen at random between nearest available Targets) and take the Protect Action. If they have any additional Actions, they must convert them into Reactions. While Protecting, the Character must intercept clashes for any Target within range, including enemies. When this Character recovers from being Staggered, immediately unpanic. | The Character can be forced to use the Protect Action on a specific Target by the Provoker. |
| 4 (Freeze) | On their turn, the Character must convert their Action(s) into Reactions. They cannot use their movement action. They cannot use the Opportunity Attack Reaction. When the Character ends their third turn under this effect, immediately unpanic. | The type (Counter, Block or Evade) of the first N reactions the Character uses are selected by the Provoker. N is equal to the Provoker’s Rank. |

### Recovering and Healing

After combat, characters normally will try to recover from their wounds and fears.

When a combat ends, characters recover all their Stagger Threshold, Light equal to `Rank`, and [`2+Prudence`] Sanity.

Characters can also rest one hour to recover based on their Rank and Stats:

| Attribute | Recovery |
| ----------- | ----------- |
| Health Points | `10 + [Fortitude*2] + [Rank*3]` |
| Sanity Points | `1+Prudence` |
| Light | `Rank/2` |

Characters can rest multiple hours, recovering these amounts each hour of resting.

## Act 7: Economy
The currency used in the City is called ‘Ahn’.

Ahn will be controlled in groups of “k”, 1 k represents 1,000 Ahn. No important or relevant product will be under 1,000 Ahn or wouldn’t have a price divisible by 1,000 Ahn.

Characters earn Ahn by completing jobs and use that Ahn for purchasing the Equipment they use on their missions.

**Consideration: About Averages**
The term “Average” will be used a lot on the following tables. This is to represent the line where most prices will be. Game Masters can and will change these prices, but price changes shouldn't deviate more than 25% from the original price, unless the piece of Equipment is exceptional or famous.

### Weapons, Outfits, and Tools
While most combatants in The City normally move with only a couple of weapons, they still need to buy that new equipment or upgrade their old ones, whether having new interests or trying to expand their options.

#### Buying Equipment
Weapons, Outfits and custom Tools are all bought following the prices in the next table.

**Ammunition**, **Explosives** and **Market tools** are present in the Item List with their own prices.

**Average cost of Equipment**

#### Upgrading Equipment
Upgrading equipment is a normal tendency between Fixers, especially those that have commissioned a custom weapon, outfit or augment. 

Upgrading equipment is a linear process, there is no way of upgrading a Rank 1 Weapon to Rank 3 without paying for the Rank 2 Upgrade first. All equipment follows the next table when upgrading, use the price of the Rank you want to obtain [if you are upgrading from Rank 1 to Rank 2, you use the Rank 2 column].

When upgrading equipment, you may use the new EP and any unused EP from a prior rank to:
- Take new Positive Effects. (Ex: Spending 3 EP to gain Haste 1)
- Increase already present Positive Effects. (Ex: Spending 4 EP to increase Inflict Burn 2 to 4)
- Change any already present and new Negative Effects freely.

As a rule of thumb, all already present positive effects are "locked in" on a piece of equipment when upgrading, while already present negative effects can be changed. Any other EP is fair game to be used as the player chooses.

When upgrading equipment, you may freely change the Clash Win/Clash Lose conditions of the present effects. 
When upgrading Outfits, any Outfit Resistances are based off of the Resistances for the Rank for purposes of Resistance Trading, and may be completely different from the preexisting Resistances before upgrade.

#### Looting the Dead and Damaged Equipment

When looting Weapons or Outfits of NPCs, their Equipment is always considered **Damaged**.

Ammunition and Tools can’t be **Damaged** and can be looted without problems.
Augments can’t be looted at all.

### Augments and Back Up Bodies
Characters modify themselves constantly in The City, with aesthetic but also combat capable prosthetics that allow them to work correctly.

If you lose a Limb for any reason (typically mutilation), you can recover the Limb by paying an average price of `200k ₳`.

#### Backup Bodies

Death is the end of a person in the City, but nothing stops a clone with the same memory, body, identity, and personality to take their place for the right price.

When purchasing a backup body, it is assumed to come with 1 Outfit and 1 Weapon of the Rank of the Back Up Body. The exact Effects of this Outfit and Weapon only need to be addressed when the character makes use of the Back Up body.

If a character goes to a Backup body of lower Rank, they have the next effects:
- Your Augment is maintained from your old rank, but you LOCK any points and effects over the capacity of an Augment of the backup body's rank and they are unusable. Once you rank back up, you do not need to repurchase those points in order to make use of your augment.
- Your skills are completely reset, where you may recreate up to 2 skills of Rank (Minimum 1), and your remaining skills of Rank-1. You gain and change skills as normal on level up.

When buying a Backup body, you need to pay the full price in advance. If you already are in possession of a lower Rank of backup body, you may purchase a higher Rank backup body by paying only the difference in cost between the two.
